# Platform Overview

<span className="badge badge-beginner">Beginner</span>

Not all social media platforms are the same. Each one has different users, content styles, and best practices. Let's explore the major platforms and how businesses use them.

---

## The Big Picture

Each platform has its own:
- **Audience** - Who uses it most
- **Content style** - What works best there
- **Algorithm** - How it decides what to show people
- **Vibe** - The culture and expectations

<div className="tip-box">
  <strong>Key Insight:</strong> You don't need to be on EVERY platform. Smart marketers pick the platforms where their audience actually hangs out.
</div>

---

## Instagram

### Who Uses It
- Mostly ages 18-34, but plenty of teens too
- Over 2 billion monthly users
- Popular for lifestyle, fashion, food, travel, and visual content

### Content Types
- **Feed posts** - Photos and carousels (multiple images)
- **Stories** - Disappear after 24 hours
- **Reels** - Short videos (up to 90 seconds)
- **Lives** - Real-time video streaming

### What Works
- High-quality, visually appealing images
- Behind-the-scenes content
- User-generated content (reposting customer photos)
- Consistent aesthetic/style

### Best For
- Visual products (fashion, food, art, pets)
- Building a brand personality
- Connecting with local communities

---

## TikTok

### Who Uses It
- Huge with Gen Z (teens and young adults)
- Over 1 billion monthly users
- Known for trends, entertainment, and creativity

### Content Types
- **Short videos** - 15 seconds to 10 minutes
- **Duets** - React to or collaborate with other videos
- **Stitches** - Use clips from other videos
- **Lives** - Real-time streaming

### What Works
- Authentic, unpolished content
- Jumping on trends and sounds
- Educational content ("Did you know...")
- Entertainment and humor
- Showing personality

### Best For
- Reaching younger audiences
- Viral potential
- Showing brand personality
- Educational content

<div className="fun-fact">
  <strong>TikTok Fact:</strong> Videos from accounts with zero followers can still go viral! The algorithm shows content based on interest, not follower count.
</div>

---

## YouTube

### Who Uses It
- All ages - the most diverse audience
- Over 2 billion monthly users
- Second largest search engine after Google

### Content Types
- **Long-form videos** - 10+ minutes
- **Shorts** - Under 60 seconds (like TikTok)
- **Lives** - Real-time streaming
- **Community posts** - Text and images

### What Works
- Helpful tutorials and how-tos
- In-depth reviews
- Educational content
- Entertainment and vlogs
- Searchable titles and thumbnails

### Best For
- Detailed product explanations
- Building long-term audience
- Search-based discovery
- Tutorial and educational content

---

## Facebook

### Who Uses It
- Older audience (30+)
- Nearly 3 billion monthly users
- Great for local businesses and communities

### Content Types
- **Posts** - Text, photos, videos
- **Stories** - Disappear after 24 hours
- **Groups** - Community discussions
- **Events** - Promote local happenings
- **Marketplace** - Buy/sell locally

### What Works
- Community building through Groups
- Local event promotion
- Customer service
- Longer text posts with stories

### Best For
- Local businesses
- Older demographics
- Community building
- Event promotion

---

## Twitter/X

### Who Uses It
- News-focused adults
- Good for real-time conversations
- Popular with journalists, brands, and tech

### Content Types
- **Tweets** - Short text posts (280 characters)
- **Threads** - Connected series of tweets
- **Spaces** - Audio conversations

### What Works
- Quick updates and news
- Joining conversations
- Customer service
- Personality and humor

### Best For
- News and updates
- Customer service
- Real-time engagement
- B2B (business to business)

---

## Pinterest

### Who Uses It
- Mostly women, ages 25-44
- Used for planning and inspiration
- Shopping-focused

### Content Types
- **Pins** - Images linked to websites
- **Boards** - Collections of pins
- **Idea Pins** - Multi-page stories

### What Works
- Beautiful, vertical images
- How-to guides and tutorials
- Inspirational content
- Product showcases

### Best For
- DIY and crafts
- Recipes and food
- Fashion and home decor
- Wedding and event planning

## Quick Comparison Chart

| Platform | Best Content | Audience Age | Vibe |
|----------|-------------|--------------|------|
| Instagram | Photos, Reels | 18-34 | Polished, aesthetic |
| TikTok | Short videos | 13-24 | Authentic, trendy |
| YouTube | Long videos | All ages | Educational, entertaining |
| Facebook | Community, Local | 30+ | Community, news |
| Twitter/X | Text, News | 25-49 | Fast, conversational |
| Pinterest | Images, Ideas | 25-44 | Inspirational, planning |

## Which Platform for Paws & Claws?

Let's think about our practice business. Paws & Claws Pet Shop would probably focus on:

1. **Instagram** - Perfect for cute pet photos and reels
2. **TikTok** - Great for funny pet videos and tips
3. **Facebook** - Good for local community and events (like adoption days)

They might skip Pinterest (not the right audience) and Twitter (too fast-paced for a local shop).
